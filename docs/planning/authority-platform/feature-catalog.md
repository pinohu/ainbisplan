# Complete Authority-Site and SEO Feature Catalog

**Status:** Required  
**Scope:** shared platform and every business plan, with business-specific applicability decisions

This catalog defines the complete implementation surface. A business plan may mark a feature `required`, `conditional`, `not_applicable`, or `deferred_with_blocker`; it may not silently omit the feature.

## 1. Business strategy and authority positioning

Every business requires:

- a specific intended audience and primary user problem;
- a defensible business purpose independent of search traffic;
- a clear authority thesis describing why the property should exist;
- a differentiated value proposition and category position;
- primary and secondary jobs to be done;
- buying committee, decision criteria, objections, urgency, and risk profile;
- productized service, software, workflow, advisory, directory, content, or hybrid model;
- offer architecture, pricing posture, guarantees, service boundaries, and exclusions;
- market and competitor map;
- overlap and cannibalization analysis against every other AINBIS business;
- merge, reposition, noindex, or retirement recommendation when differentiation is inadequate;
- measurable business outcomes and leading indicators.

## 2. Brand and design-system implementation

Each property inherits accessible semantic foundations but receives a governed, distinct expression:

- primary and secondary experience archetypes;
- brand DNA inputs and reproducible generated profile;
- typography system, fallbacks, international character support, and loading budget;
- semantic color, contrast, high-contrast readiness, and dark-mode decision;
- imagery, illustration, diagram, screenshot, data-visualization, and icon direction;
- shape language, density, spacing rhythm, editorial measure, and grid behavior;
- motion purpose, limits, pause controls, and reduced-motion behavior;
- reusable component variants and page compositions;
- responsive rules using intrinsic layout and container-aware behavior;
- uniqueness checks against portfolio siblings;
- prohibited patterns, including fake dashboards, decorative complexity, excessive cards, auto-rotating carousels, inaccessible overlays, and cloned hero layouts.

## 3. Information architecture and navigation

The platform must support, where applicable:

- audience-based entry paths;
- task-based and topic-based navigation;
- pillar hubs and spoke clusters;
- product, service, solution, industry, role, regulation, location, and resource hierarchies;
- glossary, questions, comparisons, templates, tools, data, news, and expert sections;
- breadcrumbs with accurate hierarchy;
- search, filters, saved resources, and related-content discovery;
- contextual next-step navigation;
- footer navigation covering trust, policies, contact, and high-value resources;
- HTML sitemap when useful;
- crawl-depth targets and orphan-page prevention;
- archive, pagination, faceted-navigation, filter, and parameter policies;
- information-scent and label testing;
- mobile navigation that retains access to critical content and actions.

## 4. Content and page-type system

Business plans choose and customize page types from a governed registry:

- homepage;
- product or service overview;
- individual service or workflow page;
- solution by problem, role, industry, organization type, or use case;
- regulation, standard, policy, deadline, or requirement guide;
- pillar guide and supporting article;
- glossary term and definition collection;
- FAQ and question-answer page;
- comparison and alternatives page;
- calculator, estimator, assessment, quiz, checklist, or decision tool;
- template, worksheet, policy, sample, playbook, ebook, or downloadable pack;
- original research, benchmark, survey, dataset, methodology, or annual report;
- case study, implementation example, or anonymized scenario;
- expert, author, reviewer, advisory-board, and organization profile;
- webinar, event, course, video, podcast, transcript, or media library;
- newsletter archive and subscription-preference center;
- pricing, eligibility, process, security, privacy, integration, and support pages;
- location, service-area, city, state, province, region, or country pages only when unique value and real service applicability exist;
- contact, scheduling, quote, demo, intake, or application flow;
- About, editorial standards, sourcing policy, corrections policy, AI policy, accessibility, privacy, terms, advertising, affiliate, and conflict disclosures;
- status, changelog, release notes, roadmap, documentation, knowledge base, and developer pages for software products.

Every page type defines purpose, audience, required evidence, required components, schema eligibility, internal links, conversion action, analytics events, freshness policy, and indexability policy.

## 5. Editorial authority and E-E-A-T operations

Required capabilities include:

- author identity, biography, credentials, experience, and topic scope;
- reviewer identity, credentials, role, and review date;
- editorial ownership and accountable approver;
- source-quality hierarchy and permitted-source registry;
- citation capture, verification, link checking, and source archival metadata;
- fact-check and legal, clinical, financial, safety, or technical review paths;
- first-hand experience and evidence requirements where relevant;
- methodology and calculation disclosure;
- conflicts-of-interest, funding, sponsorship, advertising, and affiliate disclosures;
- AI-assistance and automation disclosures when reasonably expected;
- who, how, and why content-production metadata;
- corrections, retractions, appeals, complaints, and change history;
- versioning, effective dates, last reviewed dates, next review dates, and regulatory freshness;
- stale-content detection, refresh, consolidation, pruning, archival, and retirement;
- originality, substantial-value, and search-result comparison checks;
- content brief, outline, evidence packet, draft, review, approval, publish, monitor, refresh, and retire workflow states;
- YMYL and sensitive-domain review rules;
- translation and localization review;
- plagiarism, unsupported-claim, fabricated-proof, and citation-mismatch detection;
- content inventory, ownership, status, traffic, conversions, backlinks, freshness, and risk dashboards.

## 6. Keyword, search-intent, entity, and topical-authority intelligence

The content-intelligence layer must support:

- user-language and terminology research;
- keyword and keyphrase discovery from approved data sources;
- branded, non-branded, navigational, informational, commercial, transactional, local, and support intent classification;
- primary query and supporting-query assignment per page;
- query-to-page mapping and duplicate-intent prevention;
- topic, subtopic, question, attribute, process, regulation, entity, and relationship maps;
- search-result and competitor coverage analysis;
- content-gap and utility-gap analysis;
- entity salience and relationship validation;
- audience-stage and funnel-stage mapping;
- local and geographic intent detection;
- seasonality, deadline, enforcement, and update-event detection;
- featured-result and search-appearance opportunity mapping;
- topic coverage and authority completeness scoring;
- cannibalization, overlap, and consolidation recommendations;
- editorial prioritization based on user value, business relevance, competitive opportunity, evidence availability, and maintenance burden;
- prohibition on creating pages merely because a keyword exists.

## 7. On-page SEO and search appearance

Every indexable page requires governed handling of:

- unique, descriptive title and main heading;
- title-link preview, truncation awareness, and duplication checks;
- useful meta description without keyword stuffing;
- canonical URL and canonical consistency;
- indexability directives;
- language and locale metadata;
- Open Graph and other relevant social metadata;
- site name, organization identity, favicon, and web-app icons;
- descriptive URLs and stable slug policy;
- semantic headings and landmarks;
- crawlable internal links and descriptive anchor text;
- image filenames, formats, dimensions, responsive variants, alt text, captions, and surrounding context;
- video titles, descriptions, thumbnails, transcripts, chapters, and VideoObject eligibility;
- bylines, publish dates, substantial-update dates, and review dates;
- key takeaways, direct answers, definitions, tables, procedures, evidence, examples, and next steps where useful;
- accessible tables, code, formulas, diagrams, and downloadable alternatives;
- snippet controls and preview policies;
- search-feature eligibility without designing content solely for a rich result.

## 8. Technical SEO platform

The shared SEO package must implement:

- server-rendered or statically rendered indexable primary content;
- HTTP status correctness and monitoring;
- robots.txt generation, testing, versioning, and environment safety;
- meta robots and X-Robots-Tag management;
- canonical generation and validation;
- XML sitemap indexes and segmented sitemaps;
- image, video, news, or alternate-language sitemap support when applicable;
- sitemap freshness, last-modified integrity, URL-count validation, and submission tracking;
- redirect registry, redirect-chain detection, loop detection, and migration support;
- 404, soft-404, 410, expired-content, and removed-content policies;
- URL normalization for case, trailing slash, protocol, host, duplicate parameters, and session identifiers;
- query-parameter, facet, sort, filter, pagination, and internal-search indexation rules;
- hreflang generation, reciprocal validation, language-region mapping, and x-default decisions;
- crawl-budget and crawl-path monitoring for large sites;
- orphan-page, excessive-depth, broken-link, mixed-content, and blocked-resource detection;
- JavaScript rendering and DOM-content verification;
- production versus preview indexing isolation;
- Search Console ownership, sitemap submission, URL inspection, indexing, crawl-stats, manual-action, security, enhancement, and performance monitoring;
- Bing Webmaster Tools and other appropriate search-engine integrations;
- server-log ingestion and verified crawler analysis;
- release-time crawl and indexability tests;
- environment-specific canonical and host validation;
- migration inventories, redirect maps, rollback plans, and post-launch reconciliation.

## 9. Structured data and entity graph

The platform must maintain a page-type and business-type schema registry supporting truthful, visible-content-aligned markup such as:

- Organization and appropriate organization subtype;
- WebSite, WebPage, AboutPage, ContactPage, CollectionPage, and SearchAction where valid;
- BreadcrumbList;
- Article, BlogPosting, NewsArticle, TechArticle, or other accurate creative-work subtype;
- Person and ProfilePage;
- Service;
- Product, Offer, AggregateOffer, SoftwareApplication, or WebApplication only for real offerings with accurate details;
- LocalBusiness and eligible subtype only for real, policy-compliant operations;
- FAQPage or QAPage only when the visible content and current search-feature rules support it;
- Dataset and DataCatalog;
- VideoObject, ImageObject, AudioObject, PodcastSeries, or Episode;
- Event, Course, JobPosting, HowTo, Review, AggregateRating, or other types only when truthful, currently supported, and policy-compliant;
- DefinedTerm and DefinedTermSet;
- ItemList and category collections;
- sameAs and entity identifiers based on verified identities.

The system requires:

- JSON-LD generation from typed content models;
- schema-to-visible-content parity checks;
- required and recommended property validation;
- entity ID stability and cross-page linking;
- Rich Results Test and schema validation integration;
- unsupported, deprecated, misleading, hidden, or self-serving markup prevention;
- monitoring for structured-data errors, warnings, manual actions, and feature changes.

## 10. Programmatic SEO and multi-site network safety

Every business and generated page must pass safeguards for:

- independent site purpose and user value;
- substantive differentiation from sibling businesses and pages;
- no doorway sites or pages;
- no scaled content created primarily for rankings;
- no city, state, industry, or role substitution pages without unique evidence and utility;
- no hidden network ownership or deceptive identity;
- no thin affiliate or copied merchant content;
- no fabricated clients, testimonials, reviews, results, experts, statistics, or certifications;
- no unsupported legal, clinical, financial, environmental, safety, or regulatory claims;
- no keyword stuffing, cloaking, expired-domain abuse, site-reputation abuse, or link schemes;
- noindex-by-default for generated drafts;
- similarity and duplicate-content analysis across the portfolio;
- topic and query collision detection;
- minimum original-value evidence before indexability;
- human accountability for sensitive and high-risk outputs;
- site consolidation when two properties cannot justify separate existence.

## 11. Internal linking and knowledge graph

The linking system must provide:

- hub-to-spoke, spoke-to-hub, and sibling-topic validation;
- entity-based contextual link suggestions;
- regulation-to-requirement-to-workflow-to-tool relationships;
- glossary and definition linking;
- product, service, resource, proof, and conversion pathway linking;
- related content based on user purpose, not merely keyword similarity;
- descriptive anchor-text governance;
- orphan-page and dead-end detection;
- excessive-link, repetitive-anchor, and link-spam prevention;
- broken, redirected, stale, and low-value link monitoring;
- link-priority and crawl-depth analysis;
- breadcrumb and hierarchy validation;
- portfolio-level cross-site linking only where editorially useful and transparently related;
- no artificial cross-link network designed to manipulate rankings.

## 12. Original utility and lead magnets

Each business requires a portfolio of high-value utility selected for its niche:

- calculators and estimators;
- compliance or readiness assessments;
- eligibility and applicability checks;
- diagnostic quizzes and scorecards;
- deadline, renewal, filing, inspection, or documentation trackers;
- interactive decision trees;
- checklists and audit workbooks;
- templates, forms, policies, notices, scripts, and sample packs;
- document generators and guided workflows;
- comparison matrices and vendor-evaluation tools;
- glossaries and regulation maps;
- benchmark reports, datasets, original studies, and methodology pages;
- implementation playbooks, ebooks, courses, webinars, and email series;
- personalized results with transparent assumptions;
- ungated useful value plus intentional gated upgrades;
- accessible, printable, downloadable, and mobile-friendly outputs;
- disclaimers and domain review appropriate to the tool’s consequences.

Lead magnets must correspond to the visitor’s current intent and may not be generic downloads attached indiscriminately to every page.

## 13. Conversion and customer journey

The conversion system includes:

- business-specific primary, secondary, and tertiary calls to action;
- contextual CTAs aligned with page intent and user readiness;
- scheduling, quote, demo, eligibility, intake, assessment, download, subscribe, start, resume, preview, verify, export, or purchase flows as applicable;
- persistent but non-obstructive actions;
- transparent pricing, scope, eligibility, preparation, process, timeline, and next steps;
- trust evidence next to consequential decisions;
- multi-step forms with progress, save/resume, error recovery, and confirmation;
- lead routing, qualification, deduplication, enrichment, consent, and ownership;
- CRM and customer-portal integration;
- abandoned-flow recovery consistent with consent;
- conversion copy and form accessibility;
- no fake urgency, deceptive scarcity, confirm-shaming, forced continuity, disguised ads, or preselected consent.

## 14. Newsletter, email, and audience development

Each business plan decides whether to operate a dedicated newsletter, shared vertical newsletter, or no newsletter. Supported capabilities include:

- audience promise and editorial positioning;
- topic and preference segmentation;
- source-page, intent, lead-magnet, and campaign attribution;
- double opt-in or legally appropriate consent flow;
- preference center, frequency control, unsubscribe, suppression, and deletion;
- welcome, education, deadline, update, nurture, event, and re-engagement sequences;
- newsletter archive with indexability decisions;
- deliverability, authentication, complaint, bounce, and list-health monitoring;
- behavioral triggers without sensitive or prohibited profiling;
- content-to-email reuse with editorial review;
- referral and partnership growth programs;
- conversion measurement beyond opens.

## 15. Local, multi-location, national, and international SEO

The geographic strategy is selected per business:

- global, national, state/province, regional, local, service-area, or multi-location model;
- verified service availability and jurisdiction boundaries;
- unique location content supported by local expertise, requirements, examples, data, service details, and resources;
- Google Business Profile or other listing only when the business is actually eligible;
- NAP and identity consistency;
- location and practitioner profiles;
- local reviews and response workflow;
- local citations and community partnerships;
- map and directions only for real locations;
- no virtual-office, fake-location, or city-keyword doorway strategy;
- hreflang, translation, localization, currency, units, dates, legal terms, and regional content for international properties;
- locale-specific research and reviewer requirements.

## 16. Accessibility and inclusive design

WCAG 2.2 AA is the minimum release standard. The implementation includes:

- semantic structure, landmarks, headings, lists, tables, and controls;
- keyboard access and logical focus order;
- visible focus that is not obscured;
- minimum target-size handling;
- text and non-text contrast;
- zoom, reflow, orientation, and responsive behavior;
- labels, instructions, errors, summaries, status announcements, and recovery;
- accessible authentication and avoidance of unnecessary cognitive tests;
- reduced motion, pause, stop, hide, and no flashing hazards;
- captions, transcripts, audio description decisions, and media controls;
- alt text and complex-image descriptions;
- accessible charts, diagrams, maps, equations, code, and files;
- language identification and plain-language alternatives;
- consistent help and redundant-entry reduction;
- screen-reader and assistive-technology test matrix;
- automated, manual, and user testing;
- accessibility statement, feedback route, triage, and remediation SLA policy.

## 17. Performance and page experience

The platform must implement:

- template-specific performance budgets;
- Core Web Vitals measurement in lab and real-user data;
- LCP, INP, CLS, TTFB, FCP, and resource-weight monitoring;
- server rendering, caching, CDN, compression, and connection optimization;
- critical CSS and controlled JavaScript hydration;
- third-party script registry and budget;
- responsive image generation, modern formats, dimensions, and priority rules;
- font subsetting, preload decisions, fallback metrics, and layout stability;
- lazy loading that does not delay primary comprehension;
- prefetch and preconnect only when evidence supports them;
- low-end mobile, constrained-network, and international performance testing;
- performance regression blocking in CI and production monitoring;
- no animation or visual treatment that compromises usability or speed.

Capabilities include:

- consent-aware analytics and server-side options;
- page, content, tool, form, lead, email, conversion, retention, and revenue events;
- source, campaign, content, query, lead-magnet, and assisted-conversion attribution;
- first-party identity rules and privacy boundaries;
- Search Console, crawl, indexation, ranking, click-through, and query cohorts;
- branded versus non-branded search analysis;
- content engagement, completion, helpfulness, and return behavior;

- secure forms, uploads, storage, transport, retention, deletion, and access control;
- bot, spam, abuse, account, and user-generated-content controls;
- privacy inventory, purpose limitation, data minimization, consent, preference, access, correction, and deletion workflows;
- cookie and tracker governance;
- jurisdiction-appropriate legal review;

- incident response, breach handling, status communication, rollback, and evidence retention;
- special controls for clinical, legal, financial, identity, children’s, employment, housing, and other sensitive data.

## 21. Operations, monitoring, and lifecycle management

Every business requires:

- owner, domain reviewer, technical owner, editorial owner, and escalation route;
- source blueprint and plan-version traceability;
- build, deploy, catalog, runtime, sitemap, indexation, and monitoring status;
- content calendar and review calendar;
- regulatory and source-change monitoring;
- uptime, error, performance, accessibility, form, email, and analytics monitoring;

- dependency, browser, framework, and package maintenance;
- quarterly or risk-based authority audits;
- retirement, consolidation, migration, redirect, and archive plans;
- portfolio dashboard showing evidence-based completion rather than optimistic status.

## 22. AI-native authoring and automation

Automation must be transparent and governed:

- source-grounded research assistance;
- blueprint and evidence extraction;
- content-brief and outline assistance;
- draft assistance with citations and claim provenance;
- automated link, schema, metadata, accessibility, and quality checks;
- human review and approval for consequential output;
- model, prompt, source, date, and override provenance where material;
- hallucination, unsupported-claim, stale-source, and citation-mismatch detection;
- reproducible generation and diff review;
- no autonomous publication of sensitive or indexable content without passed gates;
- clear distinction between generated, reviewed, verified, committed, deployed, published, and indexable.
