# Programmatic SEO and Multi-Site Network Safety

**Status:** Mandatory portfolio policy  
**Reason:** AINBIS contains hundreds of related businesses, making differentiation, quality, transparency, and indexation governance critical.

## Objective

Automation should make rigorous work repeatable. It must not create hundreds of low-value, slightly varied, search-engine-first properties. Every publicly indexable business must be independently useful to its intended audience and must justify why a separate site, brand, and content graph improves the user experience.

## Portfolio risk model

The portfolio has elevated exposure to:

- scaled content abuse;
- doorway sites and pages;
- near-duplicate brands and layouts;
- keyword and topic cannibalization;
- location, role, industry, regulation, or product substitution pages;
- unsupported regulatory or professional claims;
- fabricated or implied expertise and proof;
- artificial cross-linking;
- stale laws, standards, deadlines, and forms;
- indexation of incomplete generated sites;
- fragmented authority across sites that should be consolidated;
- inconsistent ownership, disclosure, privacy, accessibility, and security.

These risks are treated as architectural concerns, not editorial afterthoughts.

## Independent-purpose gate

Every business must demonstrate:

1. a real audience that would benefit if search traffic did not exist;
2. a clearly bounded problem or workflow;
3. an offer, product, service, dataset, tool, expertise, or operating model distinct from sibling businesses;
4. a content and utility strategy that is not a renamed copy of another site;
5. a credible reason to use a separate domain or property rather than a page within another site;
6. ownership and accountability;
7. evidence that its launch content will be substantial and maintained.

Failure triggers `consolidate`, `reposition`, or `remain_noindex` rather than automatic publication.

## Portfolio similarity model

The programmatic-quality package compares every business across:

- normalized title and slug;
- audience;
- job to be done;
- triggering event;
- law, regulation, standard, agency, form, document, or deadline;
- product and service deliverables;
- geographic scope;
- primary and secondary queries;
- proposed URL and content types;
- tools and lead magnets;
- claims, proof, and methodology;
- brand DNA, color, typography, layout, imagery, and motion;
- page-section order and repeated copy;
- internal and cross-site links.

Similarity is not itself failure. Undisclosed or unjustified similarity is. Plans must explain differences and users must receive materially distinct value.

## Query and topic ownership

The portfolio maintains a registry assigning:

- a primary business owner for each high-value query cluster;
- supporting business relationships when multiple businesses legitimately address different aspects;
- canonical explanatory resources where duplication would not help users;
- cross-site links only when editorially useful;
- consolidation or noindex recommendations for collisions.

A new plan cannot claim a query or topic already assigned without documenting a distinct intent, audience, jurisdiction, workflow, or result.

## Page creation gate

A proposed page is created only when all applicable evidence exists:

- identifiable user intent;
- unique purpose within the business and portfolio;
- sufficient source and subject-matter support;
- substantive value beyond summarizing existing pages;
- a defined action, decision, learning outcome, or utility;
- a clear maintenance owner and review trigger;
- a valid internal-link position;
- a truthful schema and indexability profile;
- no better existing page to update, expand, or consolidate.

Keyword volume alone never authorizes a page.

## Location and jurisdiction pages

Location pages are permitted only when the location changes meaningful content, such as:

- applicable laws, agencies, forms, deadlines, definitions, or processes;
- actual service availability or delivery method;
- local experts, offices, practitioners, partners, or support;
- local data, examples, pricing, resources, events, or community needs;
- unique user questions and decisions.

The following are prohibited:

- changing only the city, state, province, or country name;
- listing many locations without real service or expertise;
- virtual-office or fake-location representation;
- pages that funnel all users to an unrelated destination;
- duplicated text with token substitutions;
- unsupported LocalBusiness markup or listings.

## Evidence and originality gate

Indexable content must demonstrate one or more forms of original value:

- first-hand professional or operational experience;
- original research, analysis, benchmark, or dataset;
- a useful synthesis with substantial added insight;
- a transparent methodology or framework;
- a calculator, assessment, decision tree, checklist, template, or workflow;
- documented examples, scenarios, implementation lessons, or case evidence;
- qualified expert interpretation;
- a uniquely maintained reference, map, timeline, comparison, or tracker.

Simply rewriting official or competitor material does not satisfy the gate.

## Claim integrity

Claims are classified as:

- factual;
- legal or regulatory;
- clinical or safety;
- financial or economic;
- performance or outcome;
- comparative;
- credential or affiliation;
- customer proof;
- estimate or projection;
- opinion or recommendation.

Each class has required evidence and reviewer rules. Claims without evidence remain unpublished or are explicitly labeled as assumptions, illustrations, estimates, or unresolved questions.

The portfolio prohibits fabricated:

- clients;
- testimonials;
- reviews;
- case studies;
- experts;
- credentials;
- statistics;
- success rates;
- savings;
- timelines;
- guarantees;
- partnerships;
- certifications;
- regulatory approval.

Pre-launch proof slots must be visibly and machine-detectably labeled as placeholders or illustrative material.

## Indexation-by-evidence

Generated sites default to `private_build` or `public_noindex`. An automated deployment cannot make a site indexable.

Indexation requires evidence for:

- independent purpose;
- research packet;
- domain review where required;
- launch content completeness;
- original utility;
- authorship and trust pages;
- technical SEO and structured-data validation;
- accessibility and performance;
- privacy, security, and legal review;
- analytics and monitoring;
- portfolio collision checks;
- approved sitemap inclusion.

Removing `noindex` is a controlled release action with an owner and audit record.

## Cross-site linking policy

Cross-site links must:

- help the user complete a related task or understand a relevant concept;
- use transparent, descriptive context;
- avoid repetitive exact-match anchors;
- avoid sitewide reciprocal patterns created for ranking manipulation;
- reflect real product, organization, source, or content relationships;
- be reviewed during collision and consolidation audits.

The system must not produce a private link network disguised as editorial recommendations.

## Ownership and disclosure

Every property must clearly disclose:

- responsible organization or owner;
- contact and support routes;
- editorial and sourcing standards;
- how content and tools are created;
- use of automation or AI when reasonably expected;
- advertising, affiliate, sponsorship, and conflicts;
- privacy, terms, accessibility, and corrections routes;
- professional-advice limitations and jurisdictional boundaries.

Portfolio relationships should be disclosed when material to trust or interpretation.

## Content lifecycle and freshness

Each page records:

- source dates and effective dates;
- last substantive update;
- last expert or editorial review;
- next scheduled review;
- event-based triggers such as legal changes, new forms, enforcement, standards updates, pricing changes, product changes, or source removals;
- freshness status and indexability impact.

Changing a displayed date without substantive review is prohibited.

Stale high-risk content moves to review, warning, restriction, noindex, consolidation, or retirement based on severity.

## Consolidation policy

Businesses or pages should be consolidated when:

- audiences, problems, offers, and query sets substantially overlap;
- neither can sustain independent original content and utility;
- one is an obsolete or inferior version;
- separate sites confuse customers or fragment evidence;
- authority and backlinks would be more useful in one property;
- regulatory or operational changes eliminate the distinction.

A consolidation plan includes canonical destination, content reconciliation, redirect map, backlink and analytics preservation, user communication, sitemap changes, and monitoring.

## Automated checks

The portfolio CI and scheduled audits should evaluate:

- inventory-to-plan parity;
- blueprint hash and plan freshness;
- duplicate titles, slugs, metadata, headings, and page bodies;
- semantic similarity among business descriptions and content;
- copied page structures and brand profiles;
- query and entity collisions;
- location-page uniqueness;
- source, citation, claim, reviewer, and disclosure coverage;
- visible proof-placeholder integrity;
- robots, noindex, canonical, sitemap, redirect, and status correctness;
- structured-data parity;
- internal and cross-site link patterns;
- accessibility, performance, security, and privacy blockers;
- thin, orphaned, stale, or unsupported pages;
- indexable URLs missing approval evidence.

## Human review requirements

Automation may recommend, flag, and scaffold. Human reviewers decide:

- whether a business has an independent purpose;
- whether differentiation is meaningful;
- whether original value is sufficient;
- whether claims are supported;
- whether domain-sensitive guidance is safe and appropriate;
- whether a site should be indexable, consolidated, restricted, or retired.

## Portfolio compliance states

Each business receives one state:

- `unassessed`;
- `scaffolded`;
- `research_required`;
- `differentiation_required`;
- `domain_review_required`;
- `implementation_ready`;
- `private_build`;
- `public_noindex`;
- `indexable_candidate`;
- `indexable`;
- `restricted`;
- `consolidation_required`;
- `retired`.

State changes require evidence and are visible in the portfolio console.

## Release principle

The safest portfolio is not the portfolio with the greatest number of indexed domains. It is the portfolio with the greatest number of genuinely useful, differentiated, maintained, trustworthy businesses—and the discipline to keep all others non-indexable until they qualify.
