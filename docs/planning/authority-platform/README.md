# AINBIS Portfolio Authority Implementation Program

**Status:** Accepted planning baseline  
**Last updated:** 2026-07-19  
**Applies to:** every business in the authoritative AINBIS inventory, every developed microsite, every future blueprint, and the shared portfolio platform

## Decision

Every AINBIS business will receive a complete, individualized authority-site implementation plan generated from its blueprint and reviewed against shared platform standards. The program does not create 689 lightly varied websites. It creates a governed portfolio in which each business has:

- a distinct audience, problem, market, authority thesis, brand expression, and conversion model;
- a business-specific content and entity graph;
- original tools, resources, evidence, and expert-review requirements;
- technical SEO, accessibility, performance, privacy, analytics, and security inherited from shared packages;
- explicit anti-duplication, anti-doorway, anti-scaled-content, and cannibalization gates;
- an implementation manifest that remains synchronized with its blueprint, build, catalog, and runtime status.

The authoritative source inventory currently records 689 businesses, 38 developed builds, and 38 launched microsites. The planning system must cover all current businesses and automatically include newly created businesses as part of the mandatory inventory-regeneration workflow.

## Outcomes

The program must make every eligible business capable of becoming the foremost useful and trusted property in its niche—not merely a landing page. Each implementation should combine:

1. authoritative publishing;
2. a credible product or service experience;
3. original utility through calculators, checklists, assessments, templates, data, workflows, or other tools;
4. trustworthy expertise, sourcing, review, corrections, and disclosures;
5. excellent technical SEO and crawl management;
6. accessible, fast, responsive user experience;
7. ethical conversion, newsletters, lead magnets, and lifecycle communication;
8. measurable search, engagement, conversion, reputation, and business outcomes;
9. continuous maintenance, content refresh, monitoring, and improvement.

## Planning model

The implementation is planned at three levels.

### Level 1 — Shared portfolio platform

Reusable packages provide the rules and infrastructure that should not be rebuilt independently:

- design tokens and UI foundations;
- authority-site components and page compositions;
- content models and editorial workflows;
- metadata, canonical, robots, sitemap, redirect, and indexing services;
- structured-data registry and validation;
- content intelligence, internal linking, and entity graphs;
- programmatic quality and network-safety checks;
- local, national, and international SEO capabilities;
- accessibility and performance testing;
- lead capture, newsletters, consent, attribution, and experimentation;
- analytics, Search Console, crawl, backlink, reputation, and content-decay monitoring;
- portfolio governance, deployment, migration, and rollback controls.

### Level 2 — Experience archetype

Each business receives one primary and zero or more secondary archetypes, such as authority publishing, professional services, SaaS workflow, clinical, academic, local directory, real estate, children’s learning, AI-native, operations, premium consumer, or administration. Archetypes determine the baseline interaction model, information density, trust burden, conversion patterns, and prohibited patterns.

### Level 3 — Business authority plan

Every business receives a versioned manifest containing its unique implementation decisions:

- identity and authority thesis;
- audience and jobs to be done;
- risk, YMYL, regulatory, and reviewer classifications;
- products, services, offers, pricing posture, and conversion paths;
- topic, query, entity, regulation, location, and competitor maps;
- site architecture and page inventory;
- editorial calendar and content lifecycle;
- interactive tools and lead magnets;
- newsletter and nurture strategy;
- proof, expertise, sourcing, methodology, and disclosure requirements;
- technical SEO and structured-data profile;
- accessibility, performance, security, privacy, and analytics requirements;
- off-site authority, digital PR, partnerships, and reputation plan;
- rollout waves, dependencies, owners, evidence, and completion gates.

## Indexability principle

A blueprint may exist without being publicly indexable. Public indexation is earned only after a business passes the authority and quality gates. Each business has an indexability state:

- `planning` — blueprint and research only;
- `private_build` — implementation in progress and blocked from indexing;
- `public_noindex` — available for stakeholder or pilot review but excluded from search;
- `indexable_candidate` — all mandatory gates passed and awaiting release approval;
- `indexable` — approved, deployed, monitored, and included in sitemaps;
- `restricted` — temporarily removed from indexation because of quality, legal, security, or data concerns;
- `retired` — consolidated, redirected, archived, or removed with a documented disposition.

No business becomes `indexable` simply because a site was generated or deployed.

## Portfolio synchronization requirement

Every blueprint or business creation must trigger, in the same completion workflow:

1. slug and title deduplication;
2. source inventory regeneration;
3. launch-network catalog regeneration;
4. authority-plan scaffold generation or update;
5. count reconciliation across source inventory, planning registry, launch catalog, and runtime;
6. generated-artifact commits;
7. runtime verification;
8. catalog verification;
9. planning-registry verification;
10. completion status recording.

A business creation is incomplete until all ten conditions have evidence.

## Non-negotiable portfolio rule

The platform may automate structure, research assistance, validation, and repetitive implementation. It may not automate away the need for original value, truthful claims, expert or domain review, differentiated purpose, first-hand evidence where relevant, or human accountability. Businesses that cannot yet meet those requirements remain non-indexable.
